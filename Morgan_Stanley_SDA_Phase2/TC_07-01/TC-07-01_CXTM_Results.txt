TC 07-01: Link Failure From L2 Border Port-Channel Member to Fabric BC Node — Execution Results
Morgan Stanley SDA Phase 2 | April 9, 2026
Result: PASS


EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

TC 07-01 validates that shutting down a single LACP Port-Channel member link (TenGigabitEthernet4/0/1) between the L2 Border node FS2_L2H-1 and Fabric Border Controller FS2_BC1 does not bring down Port-Channel 40 and causes zero packet loss to L2 handoff traffic. The surviving member (Te4/0/2) absorbs all traffic at 10 Gbps (reduced from 20 Gbps baseline).

Three iterations were executed. All produced identical results: Po40 remained operationally UP, Spirent confirmed 0.000% packet loss across 440+ streams, LISP sessions maintained 2/2 established, OSPF adjacency remained FULL, and LACP re-bundled Te4/0/1 within ~10 seconds after recovery. Zero CTS denied counters throughout.

Multicast verification confirmed group 225.1.1.1 (VRF BMS1, source 10.5.28.11, RP 10.5.32.1) was completely unaffected by the single member failure. PIM neighbors on LISP0.4099 remained 2/2 UP, mroute entries preserved, MFIB hardware forwarding continued at ~1000-1044 pps with zero RPF failures, and IGMP snooping on VLAN 101 remained stable on Te4/0/20 throughout all phases. Po40 remaining UP means the entire multicast path is uninterrupted.

CLI data was collected from 4 devices (L2H-1, BC1, L2_9300-1, L2_9300-2) across 3 phases and 3 iterations — 36 CLI files total.

TC 07-01 confirms that a single LACP member failure on Po40 causes zero disruption to unicast and multicast traffic. Po40 remains UP with the surviving member, all control plane sessions (OSPF, BFD, LISP, PIM) are unaffected, and multicast group 225.1.1.1 continues hardware forwarding at ~1000 pps with zero drops.
