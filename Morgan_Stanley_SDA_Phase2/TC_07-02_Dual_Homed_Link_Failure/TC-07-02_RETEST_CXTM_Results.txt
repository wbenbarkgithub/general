TC 07-02: Link Failure From L2 Border Port-Channel to Fabric BC Node — Execution Results
Morgan Stanley SDA Phase 2 | April 9, 2026
Result: PASS


EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

TC 07-02 validates the dual-homed L2 Border topology by shutting down Port-Channel 40 (to FS2_BC1) while Port-Channel 41 (to FS2_BC2) provides full redundancy. Both Po40 members (Te4/0/1, Te4/0/2) were administratively shut on FS2_L2H-1, causing Po40 to transition to RD (down). Po41 remained RU and absorbed all traffic. OSPF adjacency to BC2 maintained FULL throughout; adjacency to BC1 went DOWN as expected (BFD-detected). LISP sessions maintained 2/2 established — BC1 session survived via alternate fabric path through BC2. ECMP restored after each recovery.

Multicast verification confirmed group 225.1.1.1 (VRF BMS1, source 10.5.28.11, RP 10.5.32.1) was completely unaffected by the Po40 failure. PIM neighbors on LISP0.4099 remained 2/2 UP, mroute entries preserved, MFIB hardware forwarding continued at ~1000-1035 pps with zero RPF failures, and IGMP snooping on VLAN 101 remained stable on Te4/0/20 throughout all phases. The multicast RPF path via LISP0.4099 remained reachable through the surviving Po41 path.

CLI data was collected from 5 devices (L2H-1, BC1, BC2, L2_9300-1, L2_9300-2) across 3 phases and 3 iterations — 45 CLI files total.
